# Appendix: ChIPseqSpikeInFree Normalization

## Discussion and Rationale
The `ChIPseqSpikeInFree` method offers a novel way to calculate scaling factors without exogenous spike-in DNA (e.g., E. coli DNA). 

**However, a critical caveat applies:**
According to the original methodology, this tool assumes and corrects for a massive, system-wide "global effect" (e.g., a total depletion of a histone mark across the entire genome). 

If the overall biological abundance of H3K27ac or H3K4me1 is expected to remain relatively stable between our conditions, forcing Spike-In-Free normalization could artificially skew the data. Currently, **CPM (Counts Per Million)** normalization provides a robust and visually reliable baseline for IGV visualization and relative peak quantification.
